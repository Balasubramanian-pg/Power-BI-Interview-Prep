# Remove Unnecessary Columns with GDPR/Privacy constraints

**Description:**
This transformation removes unnecessary columns from a table to ensure GDPR and privacy constraints are met. It helps in data anonymization and minimizing the risk of sensitive information exposure. By selectively removing columns, organizations can better protect personal data.

### Typical Scenario
In a real-world data pipeline, this transformation is crucial when handling sensitive customer data. For instance, a company may need to share customer data with a third-party service but cannot expose sensitive information like names, addresses, or phone numbers. By removing these unnecessary columns, the company can ensure compliance with GDPR and other privacy regulations while still being able to share relevant data.
