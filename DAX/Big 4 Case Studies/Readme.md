# Financial Reporting & Compliance

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization)**
EBITDA measures a company’s overall financial performance. It is widely used in financial statements to assess profitability without the impact of financing decisions, accounting practices, or tax environments. Understanding how to source and calculate EBITDA is essential for financial analysis and reporting.

**Accrual vs. Cash Basis Reporting**
Accrual accounting recognizes revenue when earned, regardless of when payment is received, while cash accounting records revenue only when payment is received. Big 4 firms often require adjustments between these methods for accurate financial reporting. Knowing the difference and when to apply each is critical for compliance and decision-making.


# Client Analytics & KPI Tracking

**Customer Churn Rate**
Churn rate measures the percentage of customers who stop using a company’s product or service over a given period. It is a key metric for SaaS and subscription-based businesses. Cohort analysis can further help in understanding retention patterns and improving customer experience.

**Net Promoter Score (NPS) Segmentation**
NPS categorizes customers into Promoters, Passives, and Detractors based on their likelihood to recommend a company. This segmentation helps businesses gauge customer loyalty and identify areas for improvement. Linking NPS to revenue growth demonstrates the impact of customer experience on financial performance.


# Budgeting & Forecasting

**Forecast Accuracy (MAPE - Mean Absolute Percentage Error)**
MAPE evaluates the accuracy of demand forecasts by comparing actual sales to predicted sales. It is a crucial metric for Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) teams to refine forecasting models and improve operational efficiency.

**Variance Analysis with Waterfall Logic**
Variance analysis breaks down the differences between budgeted and actual performance, identifying drivers such as volume and price changes. This helps businesses understand the root causes of deviations and make informed adjustments.


# Data Governance & Quality

**Duplicate Records Check**
Duplicate records can distort data analysis and reporting. Identifying and removing duplicates ensures data integrity, which is particularly important during audits and financial reviews.

**Missing Data Flags**
Missing data can lead to incomplete or inaccurate analysis. Flagging missing data allows for timely corrections and ensures the reliability of reports and decision-making processes.


# Industry-Specific Scenarios

**Retail: Sell-Through Rate**
Sell-through rate measures the percentage of inventory sold over a specific period. It is a critical metric in retail and consumer packaged goods (CPG) industries, providing insights into inventory turnover and demand planning.

**Banking: Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio**
LTV ratio compares the amount of a loan to the appraised value of the property securing it. It is a key risk assessment tool in banking, influencing lending decisions and regulatory compliance, such as Basel III requirements.


# Advanced-But-Practical Analytics

**Dynamic Top N Analysis**
Dynamic Top N analysis allows businesses to focus on their best-performing products, customers, or regions. Using interactive parameters enhances flexibility in reporting and supports data-driven decision-making.

**Time-Weighted Average (e.g., Average Balance)**
Time-weighted averages, such as average daily balances, are essential in financial services for calculating interest and assessing account performance. This method ensures fairness and accuracy in financial reporting.


# Audit & Reconciliation

**General Ledger Reconciliation**
Reconciliation ensures that subledger totals match the general ledger, identifying discrepancies that require investigation. This process is fundamental in audits to maintain accurate financial records.

**Threshold-Based Alerts**
Threshold-based alerts flag unusual transactions, such as large amounts that may require review. These alerts are vital for anti-money laundering (AML) compliance and fraud detection, enabling proactive risk management.


# Consulting Soft Skills

**Simplifying Complex Logic for Stakeholders**
Presenting complex data in an easily understandable format is crucial for effective communication. Using clear, simplified metrics helps stakeholders make informed decisions without getting lost in technical details.

**Benchmarking vs. Industry Peers**
Benchmarking compares a company’s performance against industry averages or competitors. This contextualizes results and helps businesses identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for improvement.

# Case Study Preparation

**Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) with Discount Rate**
CLV estimates the total value a customer brings to a business over their lifetime, adjusted for the time value of money. It is a key metric for valuation and strategic planning, demonstrating the long-term impact of customer relationships.

**Breakeven Analysis**
Breakeven analysis determines the point at which total revenue equals total costs, helping businesses understand when they will become profitable. It is a fundamental tool for pricing strategies and financial planning.

# Big 4 Interview Strategy

1. **Focus on Business Impact**
   Always connect technical metrics to tangible business outcomes. For example, explain how optimizing a measure led to cost savings or improved decision-making.

2. **Use the STAR Framework**
   Structure your responses using the Situation, Task, Action, and Result (STAR) method to clearly articulate your contributions and achievements.

3. **Practice Whiteboarding**
   Sketching data models and explaining calculations demonstrates your ability to think critically and communicate complex ideas effectively.

4. **Ask Clarifying Questions**
   Clarify assumptions and requirements before solving problems. For example, ask whether a metric should include returns or specify the data granularity needed for analysis.

This guide prepares you for technical and behavioral questions in Big 4 interviews, emphasizing the importance of linking analytics to business value and demonstrating problem-solving skills.
